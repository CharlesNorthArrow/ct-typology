Methodology

H3 Grid

Analysis uses Uber H3 hexagonal cells at resolution 8 (~0.74 km² avg. area). Connecticut is covered by 17,654 cells including uninhabited and water-adjacent areas.

Population

Per-cell population from WorldPop 2020 unconstrained UN-adjusted estimates (100 m raster), summed to H3 cell boundaries. Preferred over Census tract apportionment for small-area estimates.

Multifamily Housing

Share of housing units in structures with 2 or more units. Source: ACS 5-year estimates (2019–2023), Table B25024 (Units in Structure). Census tract values assigned to H3 cells by centroid-in-tract spatial join.

Employment Centers

Source: LEHD LODES 2022 Workplace Area Characteristics (WAC), total jobs field (C000). Employment centers are Census block locations in the top decile of job count (~417+ jobs). Each H3 cell is assigned the Euclidean distance (km) to the nearest center.

Urban Typology — Original

Each H3 cell classified independently using three variables and CT-specific quartile thresholds:

  • Urban Core: Pop ≥ Q3 and multifamily ≥ Q3 (19.4%), or within 1 mile of an employment center
  • Dense Suburban: Pop in 2nd quartile, or top-quartile with multifamily 25–50%
  • Suburban: Pop in 3rd quartile, or within 10 miles of an employment center
  • Rural: All remaining cells, including those with WorldPop < 1 person

Urban Typology — Rationalized

Same framework, applied to a neighborhood-smoothed population input: each cell's population is replaced by the mean of itself and its 6 k=1 ring neighbors before classification.

Two rounds of majority filter then applied: a cell is reclassified if ≥4 of its 6 ring-1 neighbors share a different type. No cells are locked — isolated parks, rivers, and other natural areas surrounded by urban cells are absorbed into the surrounding type. This reduces isolated anomalies and improves spatial continuity.

Book Access Infrastructure Index (BAII)

A composite index scoring physical infrastructure support for reaching library resources. Scored separately for personal vehicle (Car) and public transit (Transit). Higher values indicate greater access infrastructure quality. Developed by North Arrow for Read to Grow.

Boundary Overlays

Town boundaries: Connecticut county subdivisions (174 municipalities) from TIGER/Line 2022. Each H3 cell is labeled with the municipality containing its centroid; water-adjacent edge cells may be unlabeled.

School districts: Connecticut unified school district boundaries from Census TIGERweb (114 districts).
